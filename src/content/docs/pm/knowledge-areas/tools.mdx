Every project manager needs some tools to optimize and improve work while working on projects. Here we are sharing the links and short
descriptions of useful tools for project managers.

## Collaboration and project wiki:

1. [Notion.so](https://notion.so/) - notion helps you to connect your team and organize your work. You can write project specs, create your own databases,
create Kansan boards and roadmaps and much more. But it is best to work with designers.
2. [Miro](https://miro.com/) - Miro is the online collaborative whiteboard platform that enables distributed teams to work effectively together, from brainstorming
with digital sticky notes to planning and managing agile workflows. You can create infinite amount of boards and collaborate with your
team easily. If you are working with your engineers remotely, Miro is one of the best tools to help you get everyone on the same page.
3. [Confluence](https://www.atlassian.com/software/confluence) - Confluence is a collaboration wiki tool used to help teams to collaborate and share knowledge efficiently. With
confluence, we can capture project requirements, assign tasks to specific users, and manage several calendars at once with the help of
Team Calendars add-on. Confluence is a space for you and your team to keep all the knowledge)

## Task Management

1. [Atlassian Jira](https://www.atlassian.com/software/jira) - Jira is one of the most used task management tools. Jira lets you implement scrum and Kanban boards in your projects.
Jira is not used for only project management, but service, sales and HR management. You can configure your workflow - processes and
you are good to go. The best thing about Jira for software development teams is it’s integrations with tools such as, Bitbucket version
control and pipelines. It can save your developer’s time by 30%-40% percent.
2. [Trello](https://trello.com/) - Trello is one of the best tools to bring your team together. It has the best Kanban boards to work on. Trello has very good widgets
and integrations which makes life easier for teams. Trello is not specifically for software development, but you can configure it for any type
of teamwork.
3. [Asana](https://asana.com/uses/project-management) - Asana is also a great project management and collaboration tool. It helps your team keep everything in one place and track your
work.
4. [Mural](https://app.mural.co/)

## Rapid prototyping and brainstorming

1. [Balsamiq Mockups](https://balsamiq.com/) - Life is too short for bad software. When working with your team and stakeholders, you need visual artifacts to keep
shared understanding among team members. Visual prototypes work well for this goal. In Balsamiq, you can quickly drag and drop well-
prepared interface elements and make great mockups in a short time. You can use Balsamiq for mobile app sketching as well as web -
interface prototyping.
2. [Xmind](https://xmind.app/) - Xmind is a great tool for brainstorming. Whenever you want to share ideas, brainstorm and explain your point, use Xmind to
quickly draw mind - maps and other graphs.
3. [Draw.io](https://draw.io/) - You can use draw.io for making flowcharts, use - case diagrams and BPMN diagrams. It works very well online and is used
widely among project managers and business analytics.
4. [Figma](https://www.figma.com/login/) - Figma is mostly used by designers. However, it is a great prototyping tool. You don’t have to draw everything from scratch, there
are hundreds of ready materials and UI components for prototyping. In figma, you can easily share your work with your team and
stakeholders and communicate real time, comments feature helps you get feedback from other team members.
